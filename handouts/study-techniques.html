Study Rationale

November 16th, 2022

  • An optimal study technique has two major stages:

    • Step 1. \textbf{Step 1. }Step 1.  Complete a study guide that covers all of the notes and all of the examples covered in class. Ideally, you make a study guide yourself, since this is true ownership of learning. But, if time is an issue, you can use the study guide provided by the teacher instead. After this is completed, you should be fully aware of all of the material and concepts. But how do you really know if you are ready for the midtest? You need to be tested.
    • Step 2. \textbf{Step 2. }Step 2.  Complete the midreview. Write this as an actual test so that you can accurately determine the level of your understanding. (WARNING:\color {red}{ \texttt{WARNING:}}WARNING: Do not cram all of this the day before the exam; it must be spread out over a few days）

  • Strategy for the MidReview:

    • Write this as if it is a test (i.e. closed book with only a formula sheet / calculator), do multiple trials:
    • Trial 1. \textbf{Trial 1. }Trial 1.  Do your first attempt 2 days before the test, if possible. Correct it fully (using a different coloured pen). Identify struggle areas.
    • Trial 2. \textbf{Trial 2. }Trial 2.  Do this the following day. Complete only the questions you got wrong on the first attempt.
